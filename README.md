# Executive Scheduling System

## Overview

This project demonstrates how I approach executive scheduling in high-growth, fast-moving environments, with a focus on clarity, prioritisation, and proactive coordination.

A structured scheduling system for supporting senior leaders and fast-moving teams across multiple time zones, competing priorities, and high-stakes stakeholder coordination.

## Purpose

This system is designed to reduce calendar friction, improve decision-making, and ensure the right meetings happen at the right time with the right context.

## Core Principles

- Protect deep work and strategic focus time
- Prioritize high-impact meetings over reactive scheduling
- Reduce unnecessary back-and-forth communication
- Anticipate needs and act before issues arise
- Anticipate conflicts before they become urgent
- Maintain discretion around sensitive meetings and stakeholders

## Scheduling Priority Framework

### Priority 1: Critical Leadership Commitments

Examples:
- Investor meetings
- Executive leadership meetings
- External partner meetings
- Time-sensitive decision-making sessions

### Priority 2: Growth Team Coordination

Examples:
- Weekly team planning
- Project updates
- Cross-functional alignment
- Campaign or launch preparation

### Priority 3: Operational Support

Examples:
- Vendor calls
- Admin follow-ups
- Internal check-ins
- Documentation reviews

## Time Zone Coordination Process

1. Confirm all participant locations and working hours  
2. Identify overlap windows  
3. Check existing calendar conflicts  
4. Prioritize based on urgency and stakeholder seniority  
5. Offer 2–3 suitable options  
6. Confirm meeting details, agenda, and required preparation  
7. Add notes, links, documents, and follow-up reminders  

## Conflict Resolution Logic

When two meetings compete for the same time slot, evaluate:

- Business impact  
- Stakeholder seniority  
- Deadline sensitivity  
- Rescheduling flexibility  
- Preparation requirements  
- Confidentiality level  

## Example Output

Meeting: Growth Strategy Sync  
Participants: Growth Lead, Partnerships Lead, Product Marketing  
Time Zones: London, New York, Berlin  
Objective: Align on launch priorities  
Preparation Needed: Campaign notes, partner update, launch timeline  
Follow-up Owner: Executive Assistant / Operations Support  

## Summary

This system is designed to ensure that scheduling is not reactive, but a structured, proactive function that supports decision-making, focus, and operational efficiency at scale.
